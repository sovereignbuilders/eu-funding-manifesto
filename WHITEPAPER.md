# Architecting a Leverage-Native Ecosystem
### Overhauling European Union Innovation Funding for the Modern Solopreneur
**STRATEGIC POLICY WHITE PAPER · HORIZON 2026–2027 FRAMEWORK**

---

## The Invisible Constituency

The European Union funds innovation. What it does not fund — by design, not by accident — is the builder who needs no institution around them to build.

In the first half of 2025, **over one third of all new companies globally were founded by a single person.** Solo founders represent **30% of all startups founded in 2024** and received **14.7% of available capital.** The gap between their share of creation and their share of funding is not a natural market outcome. It is a structural bias — and this document is the argument for why EU public capital must stop amplifying it. *(Carta Solo Founders Report, 2025)*

The EU is not immune to this trend. Western Europe is home to approximately **9.5 million professional software developers** — a number that grew 21% in a single year between 2023 and 2024. *(SlashData, 2025)* A growing fraction of these developers are building independently, at leverage, with tools that did not exist five years ago. The EU funding architecture was not built for them. It has not been updated for them.

Navigating the European innovation funding landscape as a solo entrepreneur has historically mirrored the challenge of steering a massive container ship with a kayak paddle. While the European Union's institutional machinery has been notoriously heavy, 2026 marks a turning point characterised by structural overhauls designed to accelerate deployment. This document outlines the current state of play, identifies critical systemic impasses, and establishes the explicit structural prerequisites required to build a world-class, capital-efficient innovation ecosystem.

---

## 1. The Current Landscape: Assets & Emerging Trends

Legally, institutional scale is no longer an absolute prerequisite for securing European capital. The European Innovation Council (EIC) explicitly recognises "natural persons" — individuals with the intent to formalise corporate structures post-award — within its flagship frameworks.

### Core Financial Mechanisms Available to Solo Founders

| Funding Stream | Capital Available | Solo Viability | 2026 Strategic Focus |
|---|---|---|---|
| EIC Accelerator | Up to €2.5M grant / €10M equity | Medium-High (Requires scaling intent) | Generative AI, Space Tech, Future Mobility |
| EIC Advanced Innovation Challenges | €300,000 lump sum | High (High-risk, fast-track) | Translating breakthrough research into MVPs |
| Regional Cascade Funding | €20,000 to €75,000 | High (Low bureaucracy) | Green tech, localised digital integration |

### Established Baseline Frameworks

**The "Apply Now, Incorporate Later" Paradigm.** Founders can pitch directly to the EIC Accelerator as independent individuals. Corporate registration is legally deferred until the final evaluation stages are cleared and the grant is positioned for closure. This is an acknowledgment that the founder matters more than the corporate wrapper. The logical extension of this acknowledgment is to stop requiring founders to *pretend* they have a team before they do.

**Predictable National Cascade Infrastructure.** Programmes such as Spain's NEOTEC or the Netherlands' RVO/WBSO fiscal incentives provide the most reliable operational baselines. They deliver non-dilutive capital directly to micro-enterprises, bypassing the complex cross-border politics of Brussels.

### Active Modernisation Trends (2026)

**Radical Administrative Simplification.** The 2026 EIC Work Programme has aggressively minimised document overhead. Full-stage Accelerator proposals have been compressed from 50 pages down to 20, with evaluation cycle frequency optimised from 6-month intervals down to every 2 months.

**The ARPA-Inspired Model.** The EU is actively piloting challenge-driven mechanisms. The Advanced Innovation Challenges deploy €300,000 lump sums into high-risk, high-reward architectures, deliberately neutralising traditional compliance audits. The core ARPA logic — fund the person, not the organisation; bet on specific technical capability, not committee-approved roadmaps — is the same logic that should be applied at the solo founder entry tier. The EU has acknowledged this model's validity. It has not yet applied it at the earliest stage.

**Strategic Dual-Use & Defence Expansion.** The EIC has initiated direct equity positions in dual-use technologies, unlocking pipelines for solo software developers innovating within cybersecurity, autonomous systems, and counter-measure infrastructure.

### What the Numbers Say About Systemic Exclusion

The EU's flagship mechanism — the EIC Accelerator — recorded an **overall success rate of approximately 2.7%** across the full application funnel in 2024. *(EISMEA / Rasph, 2025)* For the October 2025 cutoff, 923 companies submitted full applications requesting nearly **€7 billion in total** — against a pool that funded 61 companies. *(EIC published results, February 2026)*

What these numbers do not show is who was filtered out *before* the application stage — the founders who looked at the system and correctly concluded it was not built for them.

**The 300-Hour Tax.** Preparing a full EIC Accelerator application requires approximately **300 hours** of work. *(STRATA EIC Accelerator FAQ, 2025)* For a solo founder without a grant-writing team, this is not a 300-hour investment. It is a 300-hour existential risk to the business.

**The Consultant Economy.** The existence of a multi-million-euro grant consultancy industry is itself evidence of structural failure. Specialist firms achieve **40% success rates** in the full proposal phase compared to the **5–7% EU average** — meaning access to the system is gated by whether you can afford to hire someone who speaks its language. *(Zabala Innovation, 2024)* This is not a meritocracy. It is a translation tax. The EU's public innovation capital is being intermediated by a private consulting market that exists precisely because the system is illegible to those it claims to serve.

## 2. Structural Impasses & Strategic Imperatives

Despite incremental adjustments, deep systemic misalignments persist. The existing frameworks fail to accommodate builders who prioritise small operational footprints paired with exponential technological leverage. Below are the four core impasses mapped directly to the structural mandates required to modernise the ecosystem.

---

### Impasse 1: The Outmoded Headcount Growth Bias

The prevailing architecture of major EU technical grants operates on an obsolete industrial-era thesis: **innovation is directly proportional to headcount expansion.** Elite technical founders leveraging large language models, hyper-automation, and specialised contractor networks to build high-ARR software operations do not fit this legacy model. Current programming artificially penalises operational efficiency, forcing builders to pitch inflated organisational roadmaps to hire unneeded personnel simply to satisfy archaic evaluation scorecards.

**The leverage economy is already here.** AI-enabled solo founders and micro-teams are building and shipping products at output ratios that legacy evaluation matrices cannot process. The EU evaluation scorecard still rewards *organisational density*, not *execution velocity* or *output per capita*.

**The capital allocation distortion is documented.** Solo-founded companies represent 30% of new ventures but capture only 14.7% of priced equity rounds. *(Carta, 2025)* EU public capital should be correcting this distortion — not amplifying it.

**The solopreneur capital problem is acute.** 89% of independent builders globally cite access to capital as a major pain point. *(Branch x Mastercard Solopreneur Report, February 2026)* The EU has public capital explicitly designated for innovation. The fact that it systematically fails to reach this constituency is a policy failure, not a market outcome.

#### Strategic Imperative: An Efficiency-First Solopreneur Framework

**The Operational Requirement.** The EU must establish a dedicated, permanent funding tier where success and eligibility are decoupled from headcount metrics. Evaluation matrices must prioritise output per capita, system leverage, and capital efficiency.

**Why This Is Mandatory.** This structural change allows lean operations to scale their global impact exponentially without forcing the artificial inflation of corporate overhead. By rewarding a high revenue-to-employee ratio, institutional capital will actively subsidise high-margin, resilient digital infrastructure rather than funding unnecessary hiring metrics for the sake of employment statistics.

---

### Impasse 2: The Multi-National Consortium Mandate

Early-stage entry mechanisms heavily mandate a strict consortium requirement. Sweden's national Eurostars funding body (Vinnova) states explicitly in its official programme guidelines: ***"Individuals or sole proprietorships cannot apply and will not be funded."*** *(Vinnova, 2026)*

This is not a bureaucratic technicality. It is categorical structural exclusion, in writing, from an official EU funding body.

To qualify for Eurostars — the EU's primary early-stage R&D vehicle — a solo operator must form a partnership with at least one independent entity from a second participating country, where no single partner controls more than 70% of the budget. *(Eureka Network, 2026)* For a solo operator, the resulting cognitive load of cross-border legal alignment, IP negotiation, and administrative management is an immediate barrier to entry — before a single line of the application is written.

A solo founder building a consortium to qualify must:
- Identify a qualifying partner organisation in a second EU country
- Negotiate IP ownership and liability across legal jurisdictions
- Align on scope, budget split, and deliverables with a foreign entity
- Absorb cross-border reporting frameworks on top of solo operations

For a founder whose competitive edge is speed and focus, this is not an inconvenience. It is a structural disqualifier. The consortium requirement forces builders to allocate critical hours to administrative synchronisation instead of refining code or engaging users.

This issue is compounded by a structural **TRL (Technology Readiness Level) Valley of Death**: clear frameworks exist for TRL 1–4 (academic labs) and TRL 6–8 (capital-intensive scale-ups), leaving solo operators at TRL 5 — working product, market validation phase — entirely stranded. Too commercial for research labs. Too lean for corporate accelerators.

#### Strategic Imperative: Sovereign-Founder Fast-Tracks & TRL 5 Bridging

**The Operational Requirement.** Institutional programming must eliminate the mandatory multi-national partner clause for early-stage grants, enabling solo applicants to access entry-level capital autonomously. This must be coupled with an explicit, ring-fenced funding envelope targeting the TRL 5 transition from raw MVP to market validation.

**Why This Is Mandatory.** Solo entrepreneurs operate on speed and hyper-focus. Removing consortium mandates immediately returns hundreds of productive hours otherwise lost to geopolitical management. Providing a dedicated TRL 5 bridge ensures that high-potential software products do not collapse simply because they are too commercial for research laboratories but too lean for corporate accelerators.

---

### Impasse 3: The Administrative Compliance Tax

A severe risk for solo founders securing traditional EU capital is the administrative friction tied to grant monetisation. Securing multi-hundred-thousand-euro awards triggers exhausting time-tracking frameworks, hyper-itemised auditing, and rigid, milestone-bound financial reporting. While venture-backed entities distribute this overhead to dedicated operational or financial personnel, a solo operator must absorb it directly. Compliance can cannibalize up to **30% of total working hours**.

**The comparison is stark.** A venture-backed company receiving an EIC grant distributes compliance overhead to a CFO, a grants manager, and external accountants. A solo founder receiving the same grant absorbs that same overhead alone — on top of building, selling, and operating the product. The compliance burden is identical. The capacity to absorb it is not.

**The system creates a perverse incentive.** Because EU compliance frameworks are designed for entities with administrative infrastructure, solo founders who receive grants often report spending more time on grant management than on the innovation the grant was meant to fund. Non-dilutive capital, in practice, becomes dilutive of the one resource EU funding cannot replace: founder time.

#### Strategic Imperative: Radical Output-Based Auditing

**The Operational Requirement.** The compliance model must completely shift away from process-based tracking — timesheets, invoices, granular receipts — toward an output-driven milestone framework. Capital deployment must run on upfront lump-sum disbursements validated retrospectively by technical milestones.

**Why This Is Mandatory.** In a solo structure, the founder functions as executive, engineer, and compliance clerk simultaneously. Automating and streamlining the compliance trail by linking success metrics strictly to explicit product outputs — deployment of production features, user verification metrics — immediately reclaims 30% of lost operational capacity. The builder's execution velocity remains unhampered, while public institutions maintain precise oversight of actual technological progress rather than creative timesheet recording.

---

### Impasse 4: Text-Heavy, Bureaucratic Evaluation Formats

The legacy application paradigm demands that technical founders dedicate months to drafting speculative business prose, marketing narratives, and multi-year financial projections. These documents are reviewed by panels that frequently lack deep contextual understanding of modern agile software architecture and internet-scale deployment mechanisms. This introduces a structural bias favouring professional grant writers over elite engineering talent.

**The consultant premium is documented.** Specialist firms achieve success rates 5–8x above the EU average. *(Zabala Innovation, 2024)* This disparity does not reflect a 5–8x difference in the quality of the underlying projects. It reflects a 5–8x advantage in knowing how to translate a project into the language the evaluation system recognises.

**The format selects for the wrong thing.** Multi-year financial projections in a sector where a product's market can shift in 90 days are not a signal of credibility. A deployed repository with 6 months of commit history, active users, and documented architecture tells evaluators more about execution capability than any prose document can. The EU currently has no mechanism to read it.

#### Strategic Imperative: Automated Code-First Micro-Grants

**The Operational Requirement.** The EU must deploy a standardised, automated protocol evaluating actual technical architecture. Applications must be driven by secure, time-bound read-access to version-control repositories (e.g., GitHub) and core technical documentation, with algorithms and programmatic peer review determining allocations.

**Why This Is Mandatory.** Traditional narrative-heavy selection processes systematically reward prose over proof of execution. A code-first assessment framework completely resets the baseline, redirecting capital toward elite builders based on real, auditable technical contributions and deployment cadence. Processing and deploying equity-free micro-grants (€50k–€100k) within a multi-week window allows the EU to compete directly with global micro-VCs and international angel networks — retaining elite technical talent inside the single market.

## 3. The Brain Drain Consequence: The Systemic Cost of Getting This Wrong

The funding exclusion of solo founders is not a contained policy gap. It is one contributor to a documented and accelerating loss of European technical talent.

**The net inflow collapse.** Europe's net tech talent inflow fell from **52,000 in 2022 to 26,000 in 2024** — a 50% decline in two years. *(Atomico State of European Tech, 2025)* Europe continues to lose more tech talent than it gains to the US, Canada, and Australia.

**The founder trajectory is alarming.** Around 18% of European-founded companies at Seed stage are already based outside Europe. By Series C, that number rises to **30%.** Repeat European founders now choose to build from the US at twice the rate they did a decade ago — from 9% to 18%. *(Atomico, 2025)*

**The funding imbalance is structural.** European VC deployed €66.2 billion in 2025 — representing only **22% of US investment** despite the two economies being roughly equal in size. *(CEPR, 2026)* The gap is most acute precisely at the early, lean, capital-efficient stage where solo founders operate.

**The direct line.** When a solo founder in Berlin, Lisbon, or Warsaw cannot access EU innovation capital without forming a cross-border consortium, hiring a grant consultant, or fabricating a headcount roadmap — and when US micro-VCs and angel networks offer €50–100k with a 20-minute call — the rational decision is clear. The EU is not losing these founders to disloyalty. It is pricing them out with its own architecture.

*"The brain drain to the U.S. is very real and it's going to really damage the future of Europe unless something changes."*
— Harry Stebbings, 20VC, March 2025

## 4. The Comparative Benchmark: What Other Ecosystems Do Differently

The EU does not need to invent a new model. It needs to observe what is already working elsewhere and replicate the structural logic.

**The US micro-VC model.** Firms like Tiny Capital, Calm Company Fund, and Indie.vc explicitly fund solo founders and micro-teams with revenue-based or equity-light structures. Deployment timelines are weeks, not months. Evaluation criteria include demonstrated execution — shipped products, active users, code history — not speculative financial projections.

**The ARPA model.** The EU's own Advanced Innovation Challenges are explicitly ARPA-inspired: fund the person, not the organisation; bet on specific technical capability, not committee-approved roadmaps. This logic already exists inside EU policy. It has not been applied at the entry-level solo tier.

**The IP protection gap — a barrier the EU has not yet addressed.** A foundational asymmetric problem not captured in existing frameworks: how does a small, agile, unprotected innovator safely inject high-value intellectual property into a massive, slow, potentially predatory incumbent without getting erased? The current EU framework — which requires solo founders to expose their full technical architecture in speculative prose documents reviewed by committees of unknown composition — creates a structural IP vulnerability with no counterbalancing protection mechanism. Any modernised framework must address this asymmetry directly, whether through NDAs, staged disclosure protocols, or cryptographic provenance mechanisms. The absence of such protection is itself a deterrent to the most sophisticated solo founders — precisely the constituency EU policy most needs to attract.

---

## 5. Conclusion: The Paradigm Shift Is Not Optional

To cultivate a hyper-competitive technology sector, European innovation policy must transition from funding the corporate framework around the builder to **funding the builder directly.** Implementing these strategic imperatives is a non-negotiable step toward ensuring that Europe remains the foundational home for the next generation of high-leverage digital enterprises.

The builders this document represents are not asking for charity. They are asking for access to a system they are excluded from by rules written before the tools they use existed. They generate high-margin, low-overhead, globally competitive digital products — the exact type of output EU innovation policy claims to want.

The mandate is precise:

**1. Decouple eligibility from headcount.** Measure output per capita, not employees.

**2. Eliminate the consortium requirement** for early-stage solo operators. Fund the builder, not the bureaucratic structure around them.

**3. Replace process-based compliance** with milestone-driven, output-verified disbursement.

**4. Deploy code-first micro-grants** of €50k–€100k evaluated on demonstrated execution, not speculative prose.

**5. Establish IP protection mechanisms** within the evaluation process itself, so that the act of applying does not weaponise a solo founder's most valuable asset against them.

These are not radical proposals. They are the minimum viable adjustments required to make the existing system legible to the fastest-growing segment of the European builder community.

Europe has the talent. It has the ambition. It has, in principle, the capital.

What it lacks is a door that this constituency can walk through.

This document is the request to build one.
